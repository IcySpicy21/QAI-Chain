\section{Conclusion}

The primary contribution of this work is a control architecture for non-stationary decentralized settings: uncertainty-gated action routing plus deterministic shielding, implemented with a compact estimator. Across stress and uncertainty-focused evaluations, this design preserves safety behavior while materially reducing model size.

Compact VQC-style modeling in this paper functions as the efficiency mechanism inside the safe RL stack, and blockchain commitments function as a deployment audit feature for multi-party governance. This division of roles clarifies the central claim: gating and shielding provide safety control, compact architecture delivers parameter efficiency, and verifiable logging provides tamper-evident auditability.

The expanded evidence package now includes stronger constrained comparisons, completed dual-environment 30-seed transfer evidence, calibration diagnostics, perturbation robustness analysis, and deployment-facing cost appendices. Core artifacts are reproducible through the provided one-command pipeline, with full regeneration available through the explicit script workflow in the reproducibility section.
