\section{Limitations}

The governance worlds are toys on purpose: no full peer churn, no endogenous liquidity games, no realistic micro-structure in the mempool. The quantum-inspired block and the audit hooks are research prototypes, not hardened production services. Latency numbers come from one workstation profile, not a sweep across accelerators.

Other risks are worth naming without bullet polish. Reward shaping can invert rankings---the random-vs-full anomaly is the clearest example---so readers should not lean only on scalar return. Some per-method slices still have modest seed counts outside the fifty-seed constrained suite. Simulator fidelity is limited for both uncertainty dynamics and network physics, and we do not yet model richer economic adversaries in mining or transaction flows. Baseline coverage stops short of a full 2022--2025 safe-RL zoo.

Corollary~2 is a bounded-perturbation statement under explicit assumptions, not a certificate that survives arbitrary sensor faults.

The Safety Gym pair buys external validity on standard constrained control, but it does not, by itself, mean you can wire this straight into a live chain. Those gaps are the roadmap, not footnotes we forgot to mention.
